Boscombe is a historical suburb of Bournemouth which developed rapidly from a small village into a seaside resort in the 19th century. It has a strong cultural history, stunning beach location, award winning parks and a rich architectural diversity including Victoriana, Art Deco, Gothic and Modernist. Boscombe pier opened in 1889 and was given a makeover in 2008 since when art exhibitions and live music performances have taken place. In 2009, the website Nothing To See Here named it Britain’s coolest pier with the National Piers Society voting it Pier of the Year 2010. To the east of the pier the seafront has its own Coastal Activity Park. Boscombe also has some pretty ‘interesting’ history.

There is the Boscombe Devil- a gargoyle located facing the Boscombe Grand Theatre (now the O2 Academy) placed there in the 1920s by the Lord’s Day Observance Society to show disapproval at the theatre’s decision to open on Sundays. Also, the Shelley Theatre, built for Mary Shelley, author of Frankenstein, is in Boscombe and is a fantastic cultural facility steeped in history sympathetically restored to former glory. Boscombe is currently undergoing a major investment and regeneration programme making it an ideal location for young people to make a life in. Bournemouth Borough Council have stated their vision of Boscombe as having … “welcoming, diverse, safe and clean neighbourhoods that people are proud of, offering positive opportunities for all and an arts and heritage inspired creative buzz.”

This has to be a fantastic place for local young people to make their home and to support this, Bournemouth Borough Council is building 11 shared ownership homes in the vibrant centre of Boscombe. Cherries Court will be a mix of two and three bed shared ownership homes prioritised for local residents wanting to be home owners but not being able to afford to buy on the open market. The development is within walking distance to the town centre, good transport links to the heart of Bournemouth and less than a mile from the beach!
